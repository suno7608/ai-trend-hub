W3C가 크로스사이트 추적에 의존하지 않고 브라우저 기반으로 광고 성과를 측정하는 Attribution API를 제안하면서 웹 측정의 미래를 둘러싼 업계 논쟁이 촉발됐다. 해당 API는 개인정보 보호를 강화하면서도 광고주들이 캠페인 효과를 측정할 수 있도록 설계된 브라우저 네이티브 메커니즘이다. 그러나 기존 서드파티 쿠키 기반 측정 방식에 익숙한 광고 업계에서는 새로운 표준의 충분성과 실용성에 대한 의견이 엇갈리고 있다.

<div class="lang-en" style="display:none">

The W3C has proposed an Attribution API designed to measure advertising performance through browser-based mechanisms without relying on cross-site tracking, sparking significant debate across the digital advertising industry. The API aims to balance privacy protection with advertisers' need to measure campaign effectiveness through a browser-native approach. However, the industry remains divided on whether this new standard is sufficiently robust and practical to replace existing third-party cookie-based measurement methods.

</div>

## Key Points

- W3C's Attribution API proposes browser-based ad measurement to replace cross-site tracking
- The proposal is designed to enhance consumer privacy while preserving advertiser measurement capabilities
- Industry debate centers on whether the API provides sufficient granularity and reliability for performance marketing
- This development is part of the broader cookieless future transition affecting all digital advertisers
