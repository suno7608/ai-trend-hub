## Overview

Google's latest move in the agentic commerce space signals its determination to become the default shopping interface for the web. By integrating Etsy and Wayfair—two of the largest online retail platforms—directly into its AI search agent, Google makes it possible for customers to browse, compare, and purchase products without ever leaving Google's ecosystem.

## What This Means

Previously, when a customer asked Google's agentic AI for product recommendations, the system might generate results, but the customer would need to navigate to individual retailer websites to complete a purchase. Now, customers can complete transactions directly within Google's interface.

For Etsy and Wayfair, this is a double-edged sword:

**Upside**: Access to Google's agentic search traffic, which could drive significant new customer acquisition.

**Downside**: Google becomes the primary touchpoint for customer interaction. Etsy and Wayfair lose visibility into customer search intent, browsing behavior, and comparison shopping patterns. The data advantage that Etsy and Wayfair might otherwise gain from customer behavior goes to Google.

## The Broader Pattern

This move is part of a larger Google strategy to own the entire customer shopping journey. Google has previously integrated shopping directly into search, Gmail, and YouTube. The addition of agentic AI creates yet another surface where Google can intercept shopping intent and facilitate transactions.

## Implications for D2C

For direct-to-consumer brands not on major marketplaces, this creates an interesting dynamic:

1. **Discoverability Challenge**: D2C brands need to be discoverable within Google's agentic AI system, which means ensuring their product data is rich, structured, and readily available through Google's systems.

2. **Customer Loyalty Opportunity**: While D2C brands may gain traffic from Google's agentic discovery, they should focus on converting that traffic into owned-channel customers who engage through email, loyalty programs, and repeat purchase.

3. **Platform Risk**: D2C brands that rely too heavily on Google for traffic face significant platform risk. Building owned channels is essential.
